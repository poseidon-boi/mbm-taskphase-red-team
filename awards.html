Awards

Special prizes

Best Measurement

We are interested in the Best Measurement award because our project relies on well-defined, quantitative methods to assess the performance of our filtration system. Each component of the filter is evaluated using reproducible experimental protocols, allowing us to measure adsorption efficiency, flow behavior, and material stability.

Our experimental design emphasizes calibration, documentation, and transparency so that other teams can replicate our measurements or adapt them to their own systems. By maintaining consistent standards across experiments, we ensure that our results are reliable, comparable, and meaningful.

Best Education

We are applying for the Best Education award because our outreach activities combine scientific accuracy with creativity to make synthetic biology approachable and engaging. Our project emphasizes the connection between biology, technology, and everyday life, helping the public understand how engineered biological systems can address real-world environmental challenges.

By presenting our work in an accessible and relatable manner, we aim to foster curiosity, awareness, and informed discussion around synthetic biology and its role in sustainable development.

Best Entrepreneurship

We are nominating our project for the Best Entrepreneurship award because it extends beyond technical feasibility to address real-world adoption and commercialization. Our team has identified potential stakeholders, including sewage treatment plants and water authorities, and evaluated how our filtration system could integrate into existing infrastructure.

We have examined the scalability of our design by considering material lifetime, performance under continuous flow conditions, and long-term usability. This analysis enables us to assess both economic viability and practical deployment.

Best Hardware

We are applying for the Best Hardware award because we designed a bio-derived, modular adsorption filtration system that integrates synthetic biology components into an environmentally relevant application. The modular design allows individual components to be tested, replaced, or upgraded without redesigning the entire system.

Our hardware enables proof-of-concept testing for flow dynamics, material stability, and adsorption performance. We will provide detailed design documentation, schematics, and assembly guides so that future teams can reproduce and build upon our system.

Best Software Tool

We are striving for the Best Software Tool award because we aim to develop a software platform that enables users to explore and apply curli fibers for diverse applications. This tool extends the impact of our project beyond organochlorine pesticide removal by allowing others to adapt curli-based materials for different functional needs.

By making this software accessible and adaptable, we support broader innovation and reuse of synthetic biology tools within and beyond the iGEM community.

Best Sustainable Development Impact

We are applying for the Best Sustainable Development Impact award because our project directly addresses multiple United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, including clean water and environmental protection. Our filtration system targets persistent environmental pollutants that are not effectively removed by conventional methods.

We present our sustainability analysis in a transferable manner so that other iGEM teams can adapt our approach to address region-specific environmental challenges. This ensures that our work contributes to long-term, scalable environmental impact.

Model

Introduction

Our goal was to assess the interactions between Curli fibers with an added Lysine tail and the MXene nanofilm. We identified that Curli fibers naturally bind to MXene, but the addition of positively charged amino acids enhances binding and extends the structure's longevity in water. This tail chain can be changed according to the use of our filter (Ahmad et al., 2020; Tantipanjaporn & Wong, 2023).

Sequence Retrieval & Addition of Tags

We obtained the sequence of CsgAs major subunit from PDB and used BLAST to find the corresponding gene sequence.

This sequence was run through SignalP to identify the N-terminal signal sequence, and the His-tag was removed along with it for structure generation.

We added a linker (2(GGGGS)) and an 8-lysine tag to the end of this sequence to form a protein with an overall structure similar to Chain A of curli fibres, but with enhanced affinity for MXene.

APBS Electrostatics

To preliminarily verify that the tags enhance the curli-MXene reaction, we used the APBS (Adaptive Poisson-Boltzmann Solver) electrostatics plugin in PyMOL. APBS computes electrostatics by solving the Poisson-Boltzmann equation around molecules to obtain electrostatic potentials, solvation energies and ion distributions. In the curli-MXene-water system, APBS was used to visualise the tag with the highest positive charge, which attracted the -OH groups of the MXene particle (Jurrus et al., 2017). This provides a way to possibly optimise electrostatic adsorption of MXene in aqueous conditions. The blue regions show positive charge, the red regions show negative charge, and the white regions show neutral charge (APBS Electrostatics Plugin - PyMOL Wiki, n.d.).

From the table, it is seen that the 8 Lysine residue tag had the most dense positive region, which, in theory, should result in the best adsorption to MXene. MXene’s APBS shows negatively charged top and bottom regions that adsorb effectively to the positive regions on curli fibers. Compared with the original csgA protein structure, the tagged structures have significantly more positive charge distributed. Since MXene is not an organic molecule, charges were added to each atom individually before applying APBS, as pdb2pqr was not possible.

Molecular Docking of Mxene to predicted structures

We performed molecular docking between the engineered curli fibers and MXene to demonstrate their binding affinity. The results show a stable electrostatic association between the MXene surface and positively charged residues on the curli fibers (Mohanty & Mohanty, 2023).

Molecular Docking Simulations:

MDS provides additional evidence of binding behaviour by providing stability of the complex in a solvent and showing how the molecules move and rearrange over time. In the case of Curli fibers and MXene, the system is electrostatics-driven and solvent-driven. Docking is weak at modelling this. MDS would provide more realistic evidence that the engineered tags can remain attached to the nanofilm in real-world conditions (Shi et al., 2022).

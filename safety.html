Safety

Safety and responsibility were also important in the design of our organochlorine pesticide filtration system, which combines nanomaterials, biopolymers, and biological components. Our biosafety plan focuses on containment, immobilization of materials, and the prevention of uncontrolled release into the environment, so that no new risks are introduced.

Dual Use Research of Concern (DURC)

The project does not involve the enhancement of pathogens, the production of toxins, or genetic engineering related to virulence or environmental competitiveness. The purpose is limited to the physical adsorption and containment of the organochlorine pesticide. The biological part is only used as a structural interface, thus reducing the risk of misuse or dual use.

Biosafety of MXene-Based Adsorbents

MXenes, a type of two-dimensional nanomaterial, have been widely studied for environmental use due to their high adsorption capacity. There is evidence that the biological response of MXenes is dose-dependent, surface-dependent, and dependent on the situation, with relatively low cytotoxicity when exposed in a controlled manner (Lim, 2021; Wu, 2022). Recent research has emphasized the importance of safety-by-design approaches, particularly immobilization and containment, to reduce potential risks in environmental applications (Hansen, 2024).

In the current system, MXene nanosheets are immobilized on chitosan beads and crab shell matrices, which effectively prevent the dispersion of nanoparticles and direct biological exposure. The system adheres to the guidelines for the use of nanomaterials (Wu, 2022; Hansen, 2024).

Environmental Safety and Degradability of Chitosan and Crab Shell-Derived Materials

Chitosan, a naturally derived polysaccharide, is known for its biodegradability, biocompatibility, and use in environmental applications. Chitosan materials are widely used in water treatment and adsorption of pollutants, which has a favorable environmental profile compared to synthetic polymer supports (Thambiliyagodage, 2023).

The materials obtained from crab shells, which are chitin and calcium carbonate, form a class of materials that belong to a wider class of polysaccharide materials that are environmentally friendly. These materials have the ability to adsorb while having a low ecological footprint(Behrooznia, 2024).

Biosafety of Curli Fiber-Based Scaffold

Curli fibers, protein nanofibers produced by Escherichia coli, are primarily used for structural purposes. Their previous use as a material scaffold for the production of gels and films has been proven to be non-toxic and non-pathogenic (US20190127490A1). In the current project, curli fibers are used only as a structural anchor for adsorption materials and are intended for use in a closed filtration system to ensure that engineered organisms are not released into the environment.

Containment, Handling, and Disposal

The filtration system fixes the organochlorine pesticides through the process of adsorption, hence inhibiting the distribution of the pesticides to other ecosystems. The adsorption-based cleanup process does not generate potentially toxic degradation products. The saturated filtration system components can be extracted and disposed of following the necessary guidelines for hazardous waste.

Notebook

Project Timeline and Experimental Workflow (February-August)

Identification of Strain, Genetic Parts, and Design (February-March)

1. Research and Conceptual Design

  • Literature review on curli fiber synthesis, amyloid nanofibres, and the BIND (Biofilm-Integrated Nanofibre Display) mechanism.
  • Identification of genes of interest involved in curli production (csgA and associated machinery).
  • Selection of suitable E. coli chassis strains.
  • Design rationale for lysine-rich peptide fusion to CsgA.
  • Planning of downstream assays for fiber formation, lysine display, and material characterization

2. Dry Lab Analysis

  • In-silico design of engineered csgA-lysine fusion constructs
  • Plasmid backbone selection and construct assembly planning
  • Primer design for cloning and verification
  • Structural reasoning for linker placement and peptide accessibility
  • Computational analysis and visualization of protein structure and fiber assembly expectations
  • Planning of downstream assays for fiber formation, lysine display, and material characterization

3. Procurement

  • Ordering plasmid backbones and synthesized gene fragments
  • Procurement of E. coli chassis strains
  • Ordering reagents, media components, stains (Congo Red, Coomassie)
  • Procurement of basic laboratory apparatus if required

4. Permissions and Training

  • Institutional biosafety approval
  • Completion of mandatory laboratory safety training and assessments
  • Authorization for handling genetically modified microorganisms

Wet Lab Experimentation (Total duration: April-August)

Standardization of Chassis and Baseline Characterization

1. Identification and Confirmation of Bacterial Strain

  • Gram staining and basic bacterial characterization
  • Confirmation of strain genotype using standard molecular methods

2. Growth Curve Analysis

  • Growth curve analysis under curli-inducing and non-inducing conditions
  • Measurement of optical density at regular intervals
  • Determination of growth patterns and generation time
  • Comparative analysis between engineered and control strains

Curli Fibre Detection and Characterization

1. Detection of Curli Production

  • Congo Red staining to confirm amyloid fibre formation.
  • Visual and comparative analysis of biofilm formation.

2. Characterization of Lysine-Functionalized Curli

  • Extraction and purification of the curli fibers.
  • Coomassie Brilliant Blue staining to assess lysine-rich surface display.
  • Qualitative assessment of extracellular matrix protein content.

Data Analysis and Optimization

Compilation and analysis of experimental results.

  • Comparison between design expectations and experimental outcomes.
  • Iterative optimization of expression conditions if required.
  • Preparation of figures, protocols, and documentation for iGEM wiki.

Buffer Period (Late August)

  • Troubleshooting and repetition of critical experiments.
  • Additional characterization assays, if necessary.
  • Final validation experiments.

Implementation

System Overview

BioCOP’S Filter Implementation Plan

The BioCOP’S is a point-of-use filtration system designed to filter organochlorine pesticides (OCPs) from contaminated water using mechanical filtration and adsorption without breaking the pesticides (Behera et al., 2024; Lu et al., 2011). The contaminated water enters the system from the top, passing through a curli-PETG-cellulose screen and then flowing through a bed of MXene-coated chitosan beads. The filtered water then exits through a lower outlet positioned at about 10°, which slows down the water and allows it to interact with the filtration materials.

Dual Use Research of Concern (DURC)

The project does not involve the enhancement of pathogens, the production of toxins, or genetic engineering related to virulence or environmental competitiveness. The purpose is limited to the physical adsorption and containment of the organochlorine pesticide. The biological part is only used as a structural interface, thus reducing the risk of misuse or dual use.

Filter Architecture and Working

Curli–PETG–cellulose mesh (primary layer)

The Curli fibers are mixed with PBS and cellulose and cast over a PETG scaffold to form a porous and strong mesh. Indeed, the interaction between Curli and cellulose has been known to produce adhesive biofilms and surface-associated matrices for E. coli (Saldaña et al. 2009), and cellulose films and nanofibers have been known to be used for strong supports that can be functionalized for multifunctional interfaces (Muthoka et al. 2023, Ryu et al. 2018). In BioCOP’S, it is the first filter that traps suspended particles and distributes the flow before it goes to the adsorptive layer.

MXene‑coated chitosan beads (adsorptive layer)

At the bottom of the mesh, there is a bed of chitosan beads, whose surfaces are clothed with MXene nanosheets. Chitosan beads are known as flexible carriers and sorbents due to their substantial surface area and rich amino chemistry (Şenel & Şahin, 2024), while chitosan-based materials have been utilized in the capture of various OCPs in water (Lu et al, 2011). With the addition of MXene coatings, surface area is enhanced and tunable interfacial properties are introduced; a number of these composite films and coatings have also been explored for advanced interfacial and multifunctional applications. In BioCOP'S, pesticides that have been dissolved are adsorbed and retained onto the MXene-chitosan bead surfaces without the actual breakdown of molecules chemically, which is called removal.

Hydraulics and differential pressure sensing

In the vertical configuration with the tilted outlet, the gravitational force sustains the flow, reduces the dead zones, and helps maintain equal contact time for the water with the layers. The differential pressure sensor is positioned between the inlet and outlet, measuring the changes in the value of the flow resistance over time. An increase in the pressure drop indicates the presence of solids on the curli mesh or bead packing. This increase is measured and refers to the point when the cartridge needs to be cleaned or replaced, just like the packed-bed and cartridges for the filtration of pesticide contaminated water (Lu et al. 2011, Behera et al. 2024).

Operation, Cleaning, and Maintenance

In normal use, contaminated water is fed into the system at the inlet and passes through the curli–PETG–cellulose mesh, followed by passage through the MXene-coated chitosan bed, and finally comes out of the system at the outlet. The values of water quality analysis and differential pressure values help us understand how effectively our filter is working (Behera et al., 2024; Lu et al., 2011). On cleaning, we pass 70% ethanol through our filter system to clean the curli mesh area and help desorb loosely adhered organics from our adsorbing surface; ethanol and similar solvents are well-established cleaning and regenerating agents for chitosan-based and polymer-based systems (Şenel & Şahin, 2024). After ethanol treatment and water rinse, our system is ready for use again. Eventually, a high pressure difference or poor water quality indicates that our mesh and cartridge parts are ready for replacement (Behera et al., 2024; Lu et al., 2011)a.

Material and Geometry Optimization

Optimizing the ratio of curli and cellulose, the PETG mesh arrangement, the bead size, and the MXene concentration. This is informed by methods used for polydopamine-functionalized cellulose films and cutting-edge interfacial layer modifications (Muthoka et al., 2023; Ryu et al., 2018), which help maximize the efficiency of the fluid distribution and the fluid adsorption capacity.Pressure-based user guidance: Establishment of differential ranges to help the user readily distinguish between when to change, when to clean, and when to replace, in accordance with regular maintenance and organophosphate pesticide cleanup system principles (Lu et al., 2011 and Behera et al., 2024).End-of-life and safety guidelines: Development of guidelines for the end-of-life treatment of chitosan–MXene beads and ethanol wash liquids as pesticide-contaminated waste, based upon concerns over pesticide residues in Indian water bodies (Behera et al.,2024; Lu et al., 2011).

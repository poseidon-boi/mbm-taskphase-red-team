Lab Safety

Overview

Throughout the course of this project, we will ensure adherence to institutional biosafety guidelines and standard laboratory safety practices. All experimental work will be designed and conducted to minimize risks to team members, the environment, and the public.

Laboratory Practices

Standard aseptic techniques will be followed to prevent contamination and accidental exposure. These practices would include:

  • Regular disinfection of work surfaces using 70% ethanol
  • Proper labeling and storage of all cultures, reagents, and samples
  • Restricted access to laboratory areas
  • Prohibition of food and beverages inside the laboratory

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

All team members will wear appropriate PPE while working in the laboratory, which will include:

  • Lab coats
  • Disposable gloves
  • Closed-toe footwear
  • Safety goggles when required

Biosafety Level

All wet lab experiments will be performed under Biosafety Level 2 (BSL-2). The host cell used in this project will be Escherichia coli, a non-pathogenic and well-characterized laboratory strain commonly used in undergraduate research. No pathogenic organisms, toxins, or hazardous biological agents will be used (iGEM Safety Guidelines, 2025). Instead of using OCPs we intend to use γ-PCCH (pentachlorocyclohexane) as our analog.

Chemical Safety

Several hazardous chemicals are used in these procedures and require strict adherence to chemical safety protocols.

Congo Red and Coomassie Brilliant Blue:

  • These dyes are potential irritants and should be handled with gloves.
  • Spills should be cleaned immediately using absorbent materials and disposed of as chemical waste.

SDS (Sodium Dodecyl Sulfate):

  • SDS is an irritant to skin, eyes, and respiratory tract.
  • Handle solutions carefully to avoid splashing, and clean spills promptly.

Methanol and Glacial Acetic Acid:

  • Methanol is flammable and toxic by ingestion, inhalation, or skin absorption.
  • Glacial acetic acid is corrosive and can cause severe burns.
  • These reagents must be handled in a chemical fume hood with appropriate PPE, including splash-resistant goggles.

Waste Management

Biological waste generated during the project will be handled and disposed of safely. This will include:

  • Autoclaving bacterial cultures and contaminated materials prior to disposal
  • Segregation of biological, chemical, and sharps waste
  • Disposal through approved biohazard waste streams

Regulatory Compliance

To prevent environmental release, all engineered strains will remain confined to controlled laboratory settings and won’t be released outside the lab. The project will comply with national biosafety regulations governing genetically modified organisms (Government of India, 1989).

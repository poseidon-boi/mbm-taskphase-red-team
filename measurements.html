Measurements

Measurements are an important aspect in both wet lab experimentation and iGEM. They allow us to verify whether our wet lab components and design function as intended. Two methods will be used for measurements:

  • HS-SPME
  • DI-SPME

What is SPME?

SPME (Solid Phase Microextraction) is a sampling technique that uses a coated fiber to absorb or adsorb target compounds from a chosen sample. After extraction, the fiber is introduced into an analytical instrument such as a gas chromatograph, where the compound is thermally desorbed from the fiber for analysis (Pawliszyn, 2001).

HS-SPME

Headspace SPME (HS-SPME) is typically used for volatile compounds and minimizes interference from native liquid-phase components. Although our analogue (PCCH) is not highly volatile, a fraction of the compound can still partition from the liquid phase into the headspace, allowing effective detection without interference from other dissolved substances.

Materials Required:

Stock solution, glass vial, PTFE-coated septum, water bath, holder needle, and GC injection port (Lu et al., 2011).

Chosen Coating Fibers:

  • 100 µm polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS)
  • 65 µm polydimethylsiloxane–divinylbenzene (PDMS–DVB) (Lu et al., 2011)

SPME equilibrium will be achieved by stirring the sample for 30 minutes, during which analytes adsorb onto the stationary fiber.

Performance will be evaluated by calculating total GC peak areas for PCCH obtained from multiple consecutive extractions (preferably 5–8) of the same sample, both before and after filtration (Lu et al., 2011).

A similar trend is expected, but only for a single molecule, PCCH (C6H5Cl5).

DI-SPME

Direct immersion SPME (DI-SPME) is commonly used for semi-volatile and non-volatile compounds. This method offers higher sensitivity and simpler operation by directly immersing the fiber into the liquid sample. As PCCH is semi-volatile, DI-SPME will enable direct measurement of its concentration in solution.

Materials Required:

Stock solution, glass vial, PTFE-coated septum, water bath, and holder needle.

Chosen Coating Fibers:

  • 100 µm polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS)
  • 65 µm polydimethylsiloxane–divinylbenzene (PDMS–DVB)

The fiber will be directly immersed in the solution for 30 minutes, in contrast to HS-SPME where the fiber remains in the headspace for the same duration.

Gas Chromatography Analysis

Gas chromatography analysis will be carried out using a GC equipped with a flame ionization detector (GC-FID). GC-FID is well suited for organic and semi-volatile compounds, enabling reliable quantification of PCCH before and after filtration (GC-FID | Gas Chromatography Flame Ionization Detector, n.d.).

Compared to electron capture detectors such as GC-MS, GC-FID offers lower operating costs, reduced maintenance requirements, and avoids the use of radioactive sources. The technique separates compounds based on volatility, allowing sensitive detection of PCCH even in complex sample matrices.

Conclusion

The proposed measurement framework is designed to generate reliable, reproducible, and quantitative data supporting the functionality of our system. These methods will provide measurable evidence for the reduction of PCCH concentrations and serve as a foundation for demonstrating the effectiveness of our filtration strategy.

Proof of Concept

Our workflow systemically identifies, assembles and validates a bio-nano composite system for organochlorine sequestration. Through literature analysis, we identified proven OCP-binding candidates, biological scaffolds, and required linkers. Wet lab experiments have been designed and proposed to verify system functionality, while computational simulations optimize component interactions and help in selection of appropriate components.

Screening of candidates that bind to organochlorines

The ability of chitosan beads to bind to a wide range of organochlorines, (17 types of OCPs) found in water samples, was demonstrated in a research paper. The electrostatic interactions, dipole interactions and adsorption mechanisms (due to rough surface and pores the beads possess) contribute to this ability(Li et al., 2011).

Curli protein as a scaffold

Curli protein fibers are functional amyloid proteins that exhibit robust self-assembly and strong adhesion. These properties have led to its popularity as a major structural component of biofilms, and as the superior choice for the design of our system as well (Dunbar et al., 2019).

The use of MXene and modifications required for Curli fibers

While Curli and Chitosan particles play their respective roles effectively, they do not bind to each other naturally. This prompted the need to search for linkers or means to bind the two together. Two studies have shown the two tend to bind effectively to one common material: MXene. MXene is a remarkable 2D nanomaterial, which has shown the ability to bind to chitosan and Curli protein effectively.

MXene is represented by a general chemical formula Mn+1XnTx, where M is an early transition metal, X is either carbon and/or nitrogen, and T represents the surface terminations (-OH, -O, -F). These surface terminations tend to possess a partial negative charge, causing negative polarity. To make the binding of Curli stronger to MXene, modified Curli proteins, with primarily positive residue tags were tested computationally. Using AlphaFold 3 to obtain the structures and ABPS electrostatics plugin in PyMOL to visualise the tags with highest positive charge on them to ensure strong binding to negative surface terminations on MXene, it was concluded that the 8-lysine residue tag showed the best theoretical results (APBS Electrostatics Plugin - PyMOL Wiki, n.d.).

Molecular docking between engineered curli fiber structures, with positive residue tags, and MXene showed stable electrostatic association (García et al., 2024).

BioCops

Persistent.

Pervasive.

Still Present.

Organochlorine pesticides (OCPs) are a class of chemically stable compounds that persist in the environment even after strict restrictions on their use.

They are extremely slow to break down and do not dissolve in water, contaminating soil and waterbodies.

Once OCPs spread to the water, they bioaccumulate and biomagnify in organisms.

OCPs and OCP contamination can cause immediate and long-term damage to humans and wildlife by affecting their nervous system and reproduction.

Yet, most remediation strategies are not designed to address chemicals defined by persistence.

This challenge calls for a system that does not rely on degradation alone, but on targeted sequestration.

So, we designed one.

Our modular, bio-based platform selectively traps OCPs in acquatic systems.
